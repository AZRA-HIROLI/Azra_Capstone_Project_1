Data Quality Summary Report
Below is the verified audit and system documentation based on our execution against the live AMFI/mfapi.in endpoints.

1. Structure of the AMFI Scheme Code
AMFI scheme codes are 6-digit unique primary keys that represent precise structural combinations of financial assets.

Unlike ISIN codes (which are globally unique alpha-numeric strings representing security wrappers), the AMFI 6-digit integer maps directly to distinct execution routes of Indian mutual funds:

Option Permutations: An AMC's single fund strategy (e.g., SBI Bluechip) is split across 4 distinct AMFI codes to isolate Direct-Growth, Direct-IDCW, Regular-Growth, and Regular-IDCW.

Block Ranges: Schemes starting with 11 or 12 typically denote established portfolios created prior to 2018, while newer schemes launched during the market expansions of 2022–2026 are assigned blocks starting with 14 or 15.

2. Fund Master Metadata Limitations
A critical finding during exploration of the API is that the open mfapi.in/mf endpoint does not natively publish standard SEBI metadata fields (such as category, sub-category, or risk-grade) directly inside the index directory.

To resolve this limitation within production databases:

Fund House extraction: Derived safely by parsing the string patterns preceding "Mutual Fund" or using string tokenizers.

Category and Risk Profile ingestion: To access risk grades (e.g., Very High, Moderate) and official categories (e.g., Large Cap, Flexi Cap), your production engine must query and cross-reference the official AMFI Daily NAV Text Sheets containing SEBI category-tagged scheme headers.
